## The New Rune

Across the face of the ninth pillar, a new symbol etches itself into the stone.
Describe it slowly.
An incomplete, fractured circle.
Asymmetrical.
Uneven.
From its centre, eight jagged lines split inward — not straight, but broken.

## 🎭 Read Aloud

One by one, the novices begin speaking.
Their voices are calm.

“By my will, I abandon Glory — I seek no light beyond my own.”

The Glory symbol on the ninth pillar shatters.

Its light dims to embers.

“By my will, I forsake the Ancients — I sever the old roots that bind me.”

Another symbol fractures.
A crack runs through the pillar.

“By my will, I renounce Redemption — mercy is weakness I cast aside.” 
“By my will, I reject the Crown — I kneel to no throne.” 
“By my will, I eclipse the Watchers — I guard only what I choose to see.” 
“By my will, I abandon Conquest — I claim dominion without restraint.”

Now only two remain.

Devotion.

Vengeance.

Burning side by side.

# The Turn

Devotion finally looks toward the party.

“Even justice… can be bent.”

Then, calmly:

“By my will, I abandon Vengeance — justice bends to my desire.”

The Vengeance symbol does not dim gently.

It violently shatters.

A shockwave slams outward.

Players (Strength save DC 15 to remain standing).

The fractured circle flares brighter.

Only Devotion remains.

# The Final Severance

Devotion turns back to the pillar.

Places both hands upon it.

Then he speaks.


“By my will, I break **Devotion**.
I cast aside **Vengeance**.
I kneel to no **Glory**, nor bend to the **Ancients**.
I answer not **Redemption**, nor do I yield to **Crown**.
I guard nothing, heed no **Watchers**, and claim no **Conquest**.
I bind myself to no creed, no divine will.
I claim the power that remains when faith is stripped bare.”

At this point—

Have the Book holder continue reading.

“I am Deryn Calder, Oathbreaker.”
