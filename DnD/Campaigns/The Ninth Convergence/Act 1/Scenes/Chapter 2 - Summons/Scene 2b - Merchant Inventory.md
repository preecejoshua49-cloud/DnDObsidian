## **Weapons**

**Swords**

- “A short blade, balanced for swift strikes. Perfect when your reflexes outrun your foes.”
    
- “A long sword, sharp as sunlight, and just as heavy on careless hands.”
    
- “A slender rapier — elegant, delicate, but bites true if you know the dance.”
    

**Daggers / Knives**

- “A pair of throwing daggers — light, quick, and very convincing if aimed well.”
    
- “A curved blade for close work, sneaky as a shadow and twice as sharp.”
    
- “A small knife with a carved hilt… keeps secrets better than most locks.”
    

**Staves / Poles**

- “A sturdy oak staff — lean, simple, and hard to break if you meet trouble in the dark.”
    
- “A carved staff with faint runes — old, and whispers when you swing it.”
    
- “A quarterstaff, plain but loyal, as dependable as a companion.”
    

**Other Weapons**

- “A club with iron studs, good for persuasion by pain.”
    
- “A light crossbow, quick to load, slower to forgive mistakes.”
    
- “A warhammer, blunt and unforgiving — leaves an impression whether the target survives or not.”

## **Armor**

**Light Armor**

- “Leather jerkin, supple and quiet. Moves with you, not against you.”
    
- “Padded gambeson, thick cloth that won’t weigh you down but might save a rib.”
    
- “Studded leather, the faint glint of metal in the sun — not much, but it keeps you breathing.”
    

**Medium Armor**

- “Chainmail vest — sturdy, catches blows, heavier than it looks.”
    
- “Scale armour of overlapping iron plates, flexible enough for most manoeuvres.”
    
- “Reinforced leather with steel lining — balance between agility and survival.”
    

**Heavy Armor**

- “Full plate, polished. Makes a statement, and shields nearly all the important parts.”
    
- “Breastplate and greaves, with chainmail beneath — a middle ground for the cautious.”
    
- “Heavy reinforced pauldrons and cuirass — your enemies feel it when you move.”

## **Trinkets / Supplies**

**Food & Rations**

- “Hard bread, salted meat, dried fruit — good for days on the road.”
    
- “Lamp oil and torches — see your enemies before they see you.”
    
- “A flask of spiced wine, warming, and morale-boosting when the night is long.”
    

**Tools & Rope**

- “Rope, braided strong — good for climbing or restraining the unwilling.”
    
- “Grappling hook, iron-tipped, balanced for tossing.”
    
- “Pouch of caltrops, small and sharp — leave a trail your enemies won’t forget.”
    

**Oddities / Curios**

- “A small vial of chalk dust — marks walls without leaving your fingerprints.”
    
- “Tiny compass that wobbles oddly — not quite right, but works.”
    
- “Strange whistle carved from bone — never used, but it hums faintly if you blow.”
    
- “Pouch of dried herbs, smells faintly of fire and spice — handy for stew or spell work.”
